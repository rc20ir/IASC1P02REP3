About Alzheimer's

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects memory and cognitive function. It is the most common cause of dementia, and its prevalence is increasing as the population ages. As we age, our brains typically shrink to a certain degree in a healthy manner; however, large numbers of neurons are not lost. In Alzheimer's disease, many neurons lose their function and connections with other neurons, causing widespread damage to the entorhinal cortex and hippocampus.

Metabolic Syndrome

Metabolic syndrome is a cluster of conditions that increase the risk of heart disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes. It includes abdominal obesity, high blood pressure, high blood sugar, low HDL cholesterol, and high triglycerides.
